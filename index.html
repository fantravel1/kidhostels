FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is KidHostels?

KidHostels is a global platform that lists and verifies hostels exclusively designed for children and youth. Every listed hostel enforces a strict zero-tolerance policy on alcohol and drugs, provides 24/7 supervision, and meets rigorous security standards. We serve school trips, sports teams, cultural exchanges, scout groups, NGO programs, and families with older children.

Are KidHostels safe for school trips?

Yes. Every KidHostel is specifically verified for school trip safety. Requirements include 24/7 staffed reception, controlled entry systems (keycard, ID, or wristband), CCTV in common areas, gender-separated sleeping options, enforced curfews, trained supervision staff on every shift, youth safeguarding training, and written incident response procedures. We provide the safety documentation school boards need.

What is the zero-tolerance policy?

Every KidHostel must enforce: zero alcohol anywhere on site, zero drugs (including recreational cannabis, regardless of local laws), no intoxicated guests, no adult-only party areas, and no unsupervised access to dorms. Violations result in permanent delisting from our platform. No warnings, no second chances.

How are hostels certified?

KidHostels uses a multi-tier certification system. Gold certification means full compliance plus exemplary supervision and youth programming. Silver means full compliance with all safety standards. Provisional means a new listing currently under review. All certifications must be renewed annually through independent audits. Each listing shows last-audited dates and evidence tiers.

What age groups are served?

KidHostels serves children and youth from ages 6 to 17, with age-appropriate accommodations and supervision levels. Hostels are matched to specific age ranges, and group stays require clear adult-to-youth ratios appropriate for each age group. Independent youth travel is also supported where legally permitted.

Which cities are available?

Phase 1 covers 150+ cities across verified youth travel hubs in Europe (major school travel corridors), Japan, South Korea, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand. Phase 2 will expand to Latin America, the Middle East, Africa, and Southeast Asia. Our global target is 10,000+ cities with standardized youth-hostel certification.
